Understanding Your Score

+

The IELTS Overall Band Score is the average of the four section scores, rounded to the nearest half-band. For example, if your scores are Listening 7.0, Reading 6.5, Writing 6.0, and Speaking 7.0, the average is 6.625, which rounds up to an Overall Band Score of 6.5. üìñ Reading Module

Master the skills to read for gist, detail, and logical argument. Learn strategies for all question types to conquer the 60-minute reading challenge.

Core Reading Skills: Skimming & Scanning

Skimming

Reading quickly to get the main idea or gist of a text or paragraph. Ask yourself: What is this passage generally about?

Scanning

Reading quickly to find specific information like names, dates, numbers, or keywords. You are not trying to understand; you are trying to locate.

General Reading Tips

  • Time Management: You have 60 minutes for 40 questions. Allocate approximately 20 minutes per passage.
  • Don't Panic: It's okay not to understand every single word. Focus on the words related to the question.
  • Vocabulary is Key: A wide range of vocabulary and understanding of synonyms are crucial for success.
  • Read Instructions Carefully: Pay close attention to word limits (e.g., "NO MORE THAN THREE WORDS").
  • Practice Regularly: The more you read, the faster and more efficient you will become.

Tips for Difficult Reading Question Types

  • True / False / Not Given: Match exact facts. "Not Given" means no information in the text. Avoid guessing.

  • Matching Information: Scan for specific names, years, or keywords in each paragraph.

üìö Vocabulary & Grammar

A strong foundation in grammar and a wide range of vocabulary are essential for a high score in all four sections of the IELTS test.

Academic Vocabulary

+
  • Academic Word List (AWL): Focus on high-frequency academic words. Example: *analyze, synthesize, evaluate, significant, comprehensive*.
  • Collocations: Learn words that naturally go together (e.g., 'conduct research', 'significant impact', 'draw conclusions', 'pose a threat').
  • Topic-Specific Vocabulary: Build word lists for common IELTS themes like environment (e.g., *deforestation, sustainable, biodiversity*), technology (e.g., *innovation, automation, digital divide*), and education (e.g., *pedagogy, curriculum, lifelong learning*).
  • Synonyms & Antonyms: Practice using a variety of words to avoid repetition and demonstrate lexical range.

Grammatical Range & Accuracy

+
  • Identify Common Errors: Pay attention to mistakes with articles (a/an/the), prepositions (in/on/at), subject-verb agreement (e.g., 'The data *shows*', not 'The data *show*'), and tense consistency.
  • Use Complex Structures: Practice using a mix of simple and complex sentences to demonstrate range. Examples:
    • Conditional Sentences: "If governments invest more, public transport will improve."
    • Relative Clauses: "Social media, *which is widely used*, has transformed communication."
    • Passive Voice: "Research *is being conducted* to find new energy sources."
    • Noun Clauses: "It is widely believed *that education is key to success*."
  • Punctuation: Ensure correct use of commas, periods, semicolons, and apostrophes.
